I was intrigued by Arrow's impossibility theorem which states that for an election with at least 3 candidates, where voters are allowed to express their preferences (Unrestricted Domain), every voting system will fail to meet at least one of the following desirable criteria:

  • Non-dictatorship: There does not exist a single voter who solely decides on the outcome.
  • Pareto efficiency: If every voter prefers candidate A over candidate B, then the group ranks A before B.
  • Independence of irrelevant alternatives: The groups preference between candidates A and B should not be affected by adding/removing candidate C from the election.

    • Furthermore, Gibbard's theorem states that if a voting system is non-dictorial and there are more than two possible outcomes, the system must be susceptible to strategic voting. This means that the best way for a voter to achieve their desired outcome might be to vote insincerely in order to sway the result in their favour.

    In other words, every voting system is flawed, and so the question of which to use in a given situation is a difficult one.
