The borda count method is simple to implement and intends to choose candidates that are acceptable to a broad number of people rather than to find one that is preferred by a majority.

The system violates the majority constraint as it is possible for a candidate to receive a majority of the first place votes and still not win the election.

The Borda method is also highly susceptible to strategic voting. If voters intentionally rank their biggest competitors low on their preference list they can have a greater impact on the result than if they rank their preferences truthfully. In fact, if all voters vote strategically, the final result is very unlikely to reflect the true preferences of the voters. The Borda count also favours a candidate that runs alongside many similar candidates.
